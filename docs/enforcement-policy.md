# Enforcement Policy

**Kenya School Fee Platform (KSFP)**
Enforcement & Penalty Implementation  
Effective Date: January 2026
Version: 1.0 (Enterprise)

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes how KSFP detects, investigates, and enforces violations of Terms and Conditions, particularly fraud and misrepresentation by schools.

---

## 2. AUTHORITY

KSFP enforcement authority derives from:
- KSFP Terms and Conditions (Section 8)
- KSFP Acceptable Use Policy
- Applicable Kenya laws
- Consumer protection regulations
- Kenya Computer Misuse & Cybercrimes Act

---

## 3. AUTOMATED DETECTION SYSTEMS

### 3.1 Fraud Detection Engine
KSFP uses automated systems to detect:

**Vacancy Fraud**
- Actual enrollment exceeds posted vacancies
- Students filled > seats posted
- Capacity mismatches with enrollment

**Document Fraud**
- Duplicate files uploaded by multiple schools
- Plagiarized content (prospectuses, documents)
- AI-generated or fake documents
- Tampered or photoshopped files

**Fee Anomalies**
- Unrealistic fee structures
- Extreme fee increases (>50% year-over-year)
- Hidden or undefined fees

**Data Inconsistencies**
- Contradictory information (claims high ratings but no documentation)
- Missing critical data (incomplete profiles)
- Logically impossible data
- Conflicting admission policies

**Academic Fraud**
- Claims top performance without documentation
- Unverifiable academic credentials
- Impossible pass rates or grades

**Pattern Detection**
- Multiple complaints from same school
- Recent account changes before fraud
- Suspicious upload patterns
- Coordinated false information

### 3.2 Human Reporting
KSFP accepts reports from:
- Parents who discover fraud
- Other schools reporting competition
- Educational authorities
- Anonymous whistleblowers
- KSFP staff during audits

### 3.3 Third-Party Verification
KSFP may consult:
- County Education Officers
- School inspection agencies
- Accreditation bodies
- Alumni and parent networks

## 4. INVESTIGATION PROCESS

### 4.1 Complaint Receipt (Day 0)
- Report received and logged (immutable)
- Assigned case number
- Auto-acknowledged to reporter
- School notified of investigation

### 4.2 Initial Assessment (Days 1-3)
KSFP determines:
- Is complaint within jurisdiction?
- Does it allege platform violation?
- Is there sufficient evidence to investigate?
- What severity level?

**Severity Levels:**
- **Level 1 (Mild):** Minor inaccuracy, easy correction
  - Example: Wrong phone number
  - Likely penalty: -0.5 stars
  
- **Level 2 (Moderate):** Material misrepresentation
  - Example: Fake vacancy data, plagiarized document
  - Likely penalty: -1.0 stars, 10% fee increase
  
- **Level 3 (Severe):** Fraud or repeated violations
  - Example: Fake academic records, fake accreditation
  - Likely penalty: -1.5 stars, 20% fee increase, 7-day suspension
  
- **Level 4 (Critical):** Multiple violations or systematic fraud
  - Example: Fake identity, money laundering, human trafficking
  - Likely penalty: Permanent termination, law enforcement referral

### 4.3 Evidence Gathering (Days 4-14)
KSFP collects:
- School's uploaded documents
- Parent feedback and complaints
- Academic performance records
- Fee structures
- Enrollment numbers
- Verification documents
- Communication history

**Methods:**
- Automated document analysis
- Manual review of uploads
- Database cross-referencing
- School records request
- Parent verification calls

### 4.4 School Response (Days 15-21)
- School receives detailed allegations and evidence
- School has 7 days to respond with:
  - Explanation of allegations
  - Corrected information
  - Additional documentation
  - Refutation of claims

### 4.5 Investigation Conclusion (Days 22-28)
KSFP determines:
- Is school liable for violation?
- What severity level?
- What penalties apply?
- Should account be suspended?

## 5. PENALTY MATRIX

### 5.1 Star Rating Penalties

| Violation | Severity | Star Reduction | Duration |
|-----------|----------|----------------|----------|
| Minor inaccuracy | Mild | -0.5 | 3 months |
| Outdated information | Mild | -0.5 | Until corrected |
| Plagiarized document | Moderate | -1.0 | 6 months |
| Fake vacancy data | Moderate | -1.0 | 12 months |
| Fake academic info | Severe | -1.5 | 12 months |
| Identity fraud | Critical | -2.0 | 24 months |

### 5.2 Fee Penalties

KSFP applies fee surcharge percentages:

| Violation | Penalty | Duration | Cost |
|-----------|---------|----------|------|
| Mild violation | +5% | 3 months | 5 KES per 100 KES |
| Moderate violation | +10% | 6 months | 10 KES per 100 KES |
| Severe violation | +20% | 12 months | 20 KES per 100 KES |
| Critical violation | +50% | 24 months | 50 KES per 100 KES |

**Example:**
- School posts fake 10,000 KES fee
- Actual fee is 5,000 KES
- Violation discovered: Moderate (fee misrepresentation)
- Penalty: +20% fee increase for 12 months
- School must now post: 5,000 KES + (5,000 Ã— 0.20) = 6,000 KES
- Parents pay 6,000 KES
- KSFP stores additional 1,000 KES in escrow for parent compensation

### 5.3 Account Actions

| Violation | Action | Duration | Recourse |
|-----------|--------|----------|----------|
| Mild | Warning | - | Appeal within 7 days |
| Moderate | Temporary delisting | 7 days | Appeal within 14 days |
| Severe | Account suspension | 14-30 days | Appeal within 14 days |
| Critical | Permanent termination | Indefinite | Appeal within 30 days |

## 6. SUSPENSION & TERMINATION

### 6.1 Temporary Suspension (7-30 days)
School delisted during investigation or penalty period:
- No longer appears in search results
- No new parent inquiries received
- Existing parents can still view profile
- School notified of suspension reason
- School can appeal or correct issues
- School reinstated automatically after period

### 6.2 Permanent Termination
School account closed indefinitely:
- **Triggers:**
  - Repeated violations (3+ violations in 12 months)
  - Severe fraud (fake identity, money laundering)
  - Criminal activity
  - Human trafficking or exploitation
  - Child endangerment
  - Refusal to comply with corrections
  
- **Consequences:**
  - Account deleted
  - Data archived (for law enforcement)
  - Funds held in escrow for disputes
  - Blacklisted from re-registration
  - Public notification to parents

### 6.3 Emergency Suspension
Immediate suspension without notice if:
- Imminent danger to students
- Active criminal investigation
- Money laundering indicators
- Child safety concerns
- Extreme fraud indicators

## 7. APPEALS PROCESS

### 7.1 Right to Appeal
Schools may appeal:
- Rating penalties
- Fee penalties
- Suspension/termination
- Penalty duration
- Penalty severity

### 7.2 Appeal Timeline
- Schools have 14 days to appeal
- Appeal submitted in writing with evidence
- KSFP reviews within 7 days
- Decision made within 14 days of submission
- Total process: 21-28 days

### 7.3 Appeal Grounds
School may argue:
- Evidence is inaccurate or false
- Violation was not committed
- Penalty is disproportionate
- Circumstances warrant reduction
- Correction efforts warrant reconsideration

### 7.4 Appeal Decision
- **Uphold:** Original penalty stands
- **Reduce:** Penalty reduced (star reduction, fee penalty, duration)
- **Reverse:** Penalty cancelled entirely
- Written decision provided with reasoning

### 7.5 Further Appeal
If school disagrees with decision:
- Independent arbitration available (school pays fee)
- Arbitrator reviews case
- Arbitrator decision binding
- Process takes 30-60 days

## 8. VICTIM COMPENSATION

### 8.1 When Compensation Applies
Parents may receive compensation if:
- School committed fraud (fake vacancy, fake fees, fake information)
- Parent paid fees under false pretenses
- Admission denied after payment
- Funds not refunded by school

### 8.2 Compensation Sources
- Fee penalties collected from schools
- Escrow funds held by KSFP
- School assets (if legally pursued)

### 8.3 Compensation Process
1. Parent submits claim with evidence
2. KSFP investigates claim
3. KSFP determines liability and amount
4. Payment processed within 30 days
5. Parent receives compensation or appeals rejection

### 8.4 Compensation Amounts
- 100% refund of fees paid (if admission denied)
- 50% refund of fees paid (if admission granted but conditions misrepresented)
- Fee penalty amounts applied to compensation
- Interest on funds held (7% annual if held >30 days)

### 9.1 When KSFP Reports Crime
KSFP reports to law enforcement if:

- Money laundering suspected

## 11. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES

### 11.1 Good Faith Errors
If school made honest mistake (not intentional fraud):
- Penalty reduced by 50%
- Star reduction minimized
- Focus on correction, not punishment
- Appeal not necessary

**Example:**
- School accidentally posted old fee schedule
- Corrected immediately upon notification
- Penalty: -0.25 stars (instead of -0.5)

### 11.2 System Errors
If KSFP algorithm caused false positive:
- Penalty reversed completely
- School receives apology
- Rating restored
- Free account credits as compensation

### 11.3 Third-Party Fraud
If person other than school uploaded fraud (hacked account):
- School may request account restoration
- Investigation into account breach
- School not liable if security breached
- KSFP liability for account security

### 11.4 Rapid Correction
If school corrects violation within 3 days of notification:
- Penalty severity reduced by 50%
- Rating damage minimized
- Shows good faith effort
